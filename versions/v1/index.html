I was accepted into PRIMES in January 2017. Since then, I have worked with my mentor, Professor John R. Williams, on analyzing the efficacy of blended learning, an educational format combining traditional face-to-face teaching with computer-mediated activities.

Combinatorial Geometry: Line-Plane Incidence Bound in R4

Starting in February 2017, I wrote a paper finding a sharper upper limit to the number of intersections between lines and planes in four-dimensional space under the mentorship of an MIT graduate student and Prof. Williams. For my work I was recognized as a Regeneron STS Top 300 Scholar and a Siemens Competition Semifinalist.

Machine Learning: Identifying Pregnancy

Beginning in the summer of 2017 under the supervision of Prof. Williams, I examined the effect of pregnancy on arterial pressure pulse waveforms. Using classical machine learning methods and a deep learning technique known as Recurrent Neural Networks, my algorithm achieved 78% accuracy with real data from the Ningbo Women and Children's Hospital. Currently, I am still working to improve the algorithm's accuracy.
